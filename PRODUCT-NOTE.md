# Product Note

With two weeks, I would focus Pulse on one promise: important customer feedback reaches the right
person and always has a clear next step.

In week one, I would make the queue dependable for daily work. Routing would save atomically, and
agents would get “mine”, “unassigned”, and “overdue” views. I would add waiting-on-customer and
closed states, clearer ownership notifications, and a safer duplicate/customer-merge review.
Managers should be able to see workload and overdue items without exporting data.

In week two, I would close the loop with a simple reply workflow and one real inbound channel,
probably email. AI would remain an assistant: suggest priority and category, summarize the thread,
and propose next actions for a human to accept or reject. I would measure override rate, time to
first owner, and missed due dates before considering automatic routing.

I would cut broad integrations, autonomous replies, custom workflow builders, advanced analytics,
and CRM-style customer management. Those are attractive, but they distract from proving that the
core queue is reliable and trusted by the support team.
